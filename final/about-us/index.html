About Us

Ayodele Thomas, Ph.D.

Executive Director, Board Member

Ayodele earned her Bachelor’s of Electrical Engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology, and received her M.S. and Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Stanford University. As a National Science Foundation and Stanford Graduate Fellow, she became, in 2005, the first Black woman at Stanford to receive a Ph.D. in electrical engineering.

Since her college days serving on the national board for the National Society of Black Engineers, Ayodele’s passion has been increasing the undergraduate pipelines for minority students in the sciences and engineering and working to increase minority representation in doctoral education and the professoriate. She served as a parent volunteer for the CAAAE Greene Scholars Program for two years before becoming its Program Director in July 2015.

Debra Watkins

GSP Founder, Board Member

Debra Watkins earned a B.A. in English with minors in French and Psychology from Pitzer College in 1976. She entered Stanford University's Teacher Education Program and earned a Master's degree in Education in 1977. A second Master's degree in Counselor Education was granted in 1996.

Debra retired after 35 years in the East Side Union High School District (ESUHSD) of San Jose during which time she taught English, and put into practice her passion for closing the opportunity gap for Black students.

As of July 2007, Debra is the Executive Director of the California Alliance of African American Educators (CAAAE), an organization she founded in 2001, along with the Dr. Frank S. Greene Scholars Program (GSP).

Kim Bomar, Esq.

Secretary, Board Member

Kim Bomar earned a B.A. in Business Administration from Stanford University, and received her law degree from Harvard University. At Harvard she was distinguished with the Joseph H. Beale Prize recognizing her academic achievements.

Since Harvard, Kim has worked at Johnson, Kline and Perkins, IBM, and currently runs her own family law practice in Palo Alto, CA. She also is an active member of the Palo Alto school board.
